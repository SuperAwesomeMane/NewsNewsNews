Plans for Wasatch canyons unveiled

Image from Salt Lake Tribune

A blueprint has been put forth for future work on the central Wasatch Mountains. The plans outlined in these blueprints include a tunnel through the mountain to link Alta and Brighton, a seperate tunnel or aerial tram to connect Brighton to Park City, a train running through Little Cottonwood Canyon, major land exchanges to allow more development at Wasatch Front sky resorts, a trail network linking the Wasatch Front and Back, and Express buses from Salt Lake City International Airport to Park City.
Mountain accord has announced a few meetings for the discussion of these plans:

  • Q/A session - Feb. 11, Cottonwood High
  • Comments - Feb. 24, Eccles Center for Perfoming Arts (Park City)
  • Comments - Feb. 25, Skyline High School (Millcreeek)
All of these meetings are scheduled to start at 6 p.m.

Semi-automatic rifle announced for commemorative legislative firearm

Image from Salt Lake Tribune

Not even joking here. Utah legislators are ordering a specially manufactured edition of the AR-15, created by Tegra Arms. The firearm is carbon fiver, with the state motto "industry" along with a beehive emblazoned on the side. Additionally, a honeybee and the motto of the Utah House, "Vox Populi", is etched onto the stock.

  • The cost for legislators to buy this rifle is $650.
  • For the price of $750, 250 of these guns are available to the public, with $100 (of each purchase) going to the Gun Safety Alliance for children's gun safety education.
  • The firearm's bullets are about 1/4th inch in diameter, and travels at about 3,000 feet per second, and typically packed into 30-round magazines.
Previous legislative firearms have included the semi-automatic Glock 19, the Browning 1911 (designated the state's OFFICIAL firearm with a legislative logo on the handle itself), and a North American Arms .22-caliber revolver.
